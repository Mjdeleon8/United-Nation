What is United Nation?

The United Nations (UN) is an international organization formed after World War II, officially established on October 24, 1945. Its primary objectives include maintaining international peace and security, fostering friendly relations among nations, promoting social progress, better living standards, and human rights. The UN replaced the League of Nations and was founded by 51 countries committed to preventing future conflicts and promoting cooperation among nations.

Why is there a united nation?

The United Nations was established to promote international cooperation, maintain peace and security, develop friendly relations among nations, and to achieve common goals amidst global challenges. Its purpose is to provide a platform for dialogue, diplomacy, and collaboration between countries.

Purpose of the United Nation

The purpose of the United Nations is to maintain international peace and security, promote social progress, foster friendly relations among nations, and achieve cooperation in solving international problems.
